Our story

Sacred song for
life's crossings

We are The Threshold Weavers — five women from Paonia, Colorado who gather in the ancient practice of ceremonial song. We sing at the places where life turns: at bedsides and birth days, in grief circles and celebrations, in the tender moments of transition that ask for more than words can hold.

A threshold is a liminal space — the doorway between what was and what will be. We believe these passages deserve to be held in beauty and in song. Our voices become a container, a comfort, a reminder that you are not crossing alone.

We also carry a second calling: to open the circle wider. Harmonic group singing is powerful medicine, and it belongs to everyone — not just those who call themselves singers. We weave our performances together with communal singing, so that every gathering becomes a shared experience of what voice can do.

The medicine

Why We Sing

At life's crossings — birth, death, grief, celebration — the body often knows what to do before the mind does. Song is one of the oldest ways we've answered that knowing.

◎

At the Threshold

We sing at bedside and birthside, in hospice rooms and grief circles. Our voices offer a container for what words cannot hold — a reminder that no one crosses alone. Song is presence made audible.

◎

In Community

Harmonic group singing synchronizes heartbeats, regulates the nervous system, and opens something ancient in us. This medicine belongs to everyone. Every gathering we offer weaves choir song with communal voice — no experience needed, no audition, no performance.

◎

Trauma-Aware

We hold our circles with care and intention — creating space that is brave, accessible, and honors the full range of what people carry. We know that the voice holds history, and we meet it gently.

"Their presence was the best part of the ceremony."

We invited the Threshold Weavers to perform at our ceremony to honor the lunar new year — an outdoor event in the middle of February. Undaunted by the potential for blizzard conditions, the group enthusiastically accepted the challenge.

They arrived at our pasture and mesmerized us with enchanting songs. Each song was perfectly curated to match the mood and subject of the ritual. To introduce the new year, they chose a song about the cyclical passage of time. To welcome us to the bonfire, they sang about the flickering flames. To connect us to the pasture we were standing in, they offered a call-and-response about finding ourselves in nature.

Their voices were harmonious and rhythmic. Their tone was angelic and soothing. Even without amplification and being outdoors, they were loud enough for all to hear. Their presence was the best part of the ceremony. I will definitely invite them back next year!
Daniel & Lara Ziskin Lunar New Year Ceremony · Paonia

Opaloka Song Temple
